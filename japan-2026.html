Rough budget guide (4 people, 14 nights)
Flights: $6,000–10,000  •  Accommodation: $5,000–8,000  •  JR Pass x4: $3,500–4,000  •  Food: $4,000–5,000  •  Activities: $2,000–3,000  •  Shopping: $2,000+  •  Estimate total: $22,000–32,000 AUD depending on accommodation standard and shopping.
Money tips
  • Cash is king — carry ¥50,000–100,000 (AUD $500–1,000) per person at all times
  • 7-Eleven and Japan Post ATMs reliably accept foreign cards
  • IC card (Suica/Pasmo) — top up on arrival for trains, buses, convenience stores
  • Prices in Japan are fixed — no tipping culture at all
Japan tips & etiquette ▼
Getting around
Get Suica IC cards from a JR station machine on arrival — works on almost all trains/buses in Tokyo, Kyoto, Osaka. Reload at convenience stores or station machines.
Luggage forwarding (Takkyubin)
Send your big suitcases ahead between cities via Yamato Transport (black cat logo). Costs ~¥2,000 ($20) per bag, arrives next day. Hotels will hold them. Saves dragging luggage on the Shinkansen.
Food & dining
Convenience stores (7-Eleven, Lawson, FamilyMart) are exceptional — great cheap meals, onigiri, sandwiches, hot foods. Restaurant meals average ¥1,000–2,500 ($10–25) pp for lunch. Many places have picture menus or plastic food displays.
Etiquette
No eating while walking (except festival stalls). Quiet on trains — no loud calls. Shoes off when entering traditional rooms. Queue patiently — it's respected. Say "sumimasen" (excuse me) generously.
Autumn 2026
Late September is still warm (~22–27°C). Early October sees temperatures drop to 18–22°C. Comfortable walking weather. Autumn foliage (koyo) typically peaks late November so it's still green but beautiful.
