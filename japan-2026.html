Transport & JR Pass ▼
JR Pass — buy before leaving Australia
14-day JR Pass covers all Shinkansen legs + local JR lines. Buy from an authorised AU reseller before you leave — cannot purchase in Japan at the old price. ~AUD $900–1,000 per adult (2026).

Japan tips & etiquette ▼
IC cards (Suica / Pasmo)
Get Suica IC cards from a JR station machine on arrival — works on almost all trains/buses/convenience stores in Tokyo, Kyoto, and Osaka. Reload at machines or convenience stores.
Luggage forwarding (Takkyubin)
Send big suitcases between cities via Yamato Transport (black cat logo). ~¥2,000 ($20) per bag, arrives next day. Hotels hold them. Saves dragging luggage on Shinkansen.
Cash
Carry ¥50,000–100,000 (~AUD $500–1,000) per person at all times. 7-Eleven and Japan Post ATMs reliably accept foreign cards.
Food
Convenience stores (7-Eleven, Lawson, FamilyMart) are exceptional — great cheap meals, onigiri, hot foods. Restaurant lunches average ¥1,000–2,500 (~$10–25) per person.
Etiquette
No eating while walking (except festival stalls). Quiet on trains — no loud calls. Remove shoes entering traditional spaces. Queue patiently. Say "sumimasen" (excuse me) generously. Tipping is not a thing.
Weather — late Sep/early Oct
Tokyo/Osaka: 22–27°C. Kyoto/Hiroshima: 18–24°C. Comfortable walking weather. Autumn foliage (koyo) peaks late November — still green but beautiful.
